Red Fort
The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fort in the city of Delhi in India. It served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors for nearly 200 years, until 1857. It is located in the center of Delhi and houses a number of museums.

Constructed in 1639 by the fifth Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, the fort is named for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone. In addition to accommodating the emperors and their households, it was the ceremonial and political center of the Mughal state and the setting for events critically impacting the region.
